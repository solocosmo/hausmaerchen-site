What Chapbook is

Chapbook is a native iOS reading app published by Solocosmo Studios. It bundles thirty-one nursery rhymes, fairy tales, myths, and storybook classics — none of them the Brothers Grimm, whose tales live in the companion app, Hausmärchen — in a clear modern reading text, with gentle Junior editions for younger readers and a parent-locked Children's Mode. The illustrations are by Jessica von Braun and Jasmine Becket-Griffith. The corpus is finite and self-contained. The app does not stream, fetch, or update content over the network.

Billing

The app is a free download. The optional "Studio Support Edition" unlock opens the complete library — every tale (the free edition opens about half of them), the full Children's Mode Junior library, and the complete gallery with every plate and the artist biographies. It is a one-time, non-consumable In-App Purchase for $9.99 USD (or its App Store local equivalent). There is no subscription. The purchase is processed entirely by Apple. We do not see your payment details.

The Restore Purchases option in Settings re-reads your StoreKit entitlement and unlocks the same content on any device signed into the same Apple ID. Refunds are handled by Apple under its standard policy.

No account, no server

There is no Chapbook account. There is no Chapbook server. Your favorites, last-read position, parent PIN hash, and unlock entitlement live on the device. We have nothing to lose, breach, sell, or hand over because we hold nothing.

Children's Mode and parental responsibility

Children's Mode is a feature provided in good faith for parents and guardians who want to share the gentler tales with a child unattended. It is parent-locked behind a four-digit PIN with an escalating cooldown after three wrong tries. We make no guarantee that any technical filter is perfect; you remain the responsible adult. The Junior editions are written to remove graphic, on-page violence — but they do not remove sorrow or danger. These are old tales: some end sadly, and a bloodless, sung, or off-page death may remain where it belongs to the tale.
